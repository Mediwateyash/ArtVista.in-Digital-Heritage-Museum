Mughal Court Scene

Period: Jahangir Era (1620 CE)
Dimensions: 12.5 × 8.2 cm
Materials: Wasli paper, gold leaf, lapis lazuli, organic pigments
Location: Agra Fort, Uttar Pradesh
Description:

This miniature painting depicts Emperor Jahangir receiving Persian diplomats in the Diwan-i-Khas of Agra Fort. Notable for its intricate border design featuring European-inspired floral motifs, reflecting cultural exchanges during this period.

Cultural Significance:

Demonstrates the fusion of Persian miniature techniques with Indian storytelling traditions. The gold leaf application method shown here was later lost and rediscovered in the 20th century.

Conservation Status:

Digitally restored in 2023 | Original housed at National Museum, Delhi
